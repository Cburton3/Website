Planning is Essential

  • Apps: for free style hiking the most important apps to use are maps.me and mappy

    Maps.me is easy to use and recommended for beginners as marking important viewpoints, ruins, and water sources such as fountains and waterfalls is especially easy. Calculating distances and plotting a trek are also very user friendly and you can downloaded the maps before hand so there is no worry if you loose your gps connection. However there are many paths that are private property or simply dont exist anymore and going down the wrong one only to come back up lost several times is an absolute nightmare. for finding places

    Mappy, in addition to all of the above has the huge benefit of showing you not just all the paths of maps.me but also the offical hiking paths specially marked in red, yellow and green so that you can rest assured that those paths are taken care of (no cutting up your shins on brambles!) and well signposted. They also usually go via some viewpoints and watersources

  • Plotting your hikes: where, when and how long?

    When plotting your hikes you must take care to go via as many water sources as possible. Take water pills even if the maps mark lots of fountains as some could be turned off. Extremely fit and experienced people will go from mountain peak to peak. This is damned right stupid if your not a professional. Firstly there are few animals at the very top of any mountain, zero flora to enjoy, no protection against the sun and climbing just 400m with a heavy bag could easily be dangerous to your health in heat. Fit people can do up to 1000m but don't count on enjoying the next day cos you won't. In hot weather stick to hikes that are wooded for protection against the heat and sun.   • Additional Material for Camping

    This depends greatly for each individual. I personally enjoy a bit of comfort after lugging around a massive rucksack for 20km. As such I opt for a portable shower (weighs little) an inflatable mattress (a few hundred grams-the space is the bigger issue), inflatable pillow, sleepign bag (3 seasons is enough if your not camping in winter), biodegrabable shower gel and shampoo, towel, wet wipes (always have as spare in case there is no water and can't shower). For cooking you'll need a portable pot, a burner,light, spoon and fork (sporks are just uncomfortable and impractical in my opinion), chopsticks if you can use them (easy to transport), cup and dry food such as noodles, meatballs or any personal preference which is lightweight. I found taking sackets of Coffee or hot chocolate great increase the mood in the morning. For breakfast I recommend oats with powered milk or quaker oats which are so sweet you dont even need milk.
